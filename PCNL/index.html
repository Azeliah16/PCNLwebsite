About Us

Vision

To be a distinctive learning institution acknowledged far and wide for its innovative and flexible approaches in working with its students, stakeholders and the community to create a more just and Humane society and a more sustainable economy.

Mission

In support of its philosophy, Philippine College of Northwestern Luzon mandates itself to provide advanced quality instruction to its clientele in education, the arts and humanities, the sciences, technology, business and other related professional and technical fields; promote the advancement of knowledge through research; and help improve the quality of community life through extension work.

This undergraduate program that provides stu- dents with the knowledge and skills necessary to design, develop, and manage computer-based sys- tems and applications. The program covers topics such as programming, database management, network administration and software develop- ment. Graduates of program are equipped with the competencies to design, develop, and maintain information systems that support busi- ness operations, communications, an and data man- agement. This undergraduate program prepares students to become licensed elementary school teachers. It covers courses in general education, pedagogy, teaching methodology, child development, teach- ing strategies, and curriculum development for ele- mentary education. Graduates can pursue careers in both public and private schools, teaching chil- dren from grades 1-6. They can also take the Licen- sure Examination for Teachers to become licensed teachers in the Philippines. Graduates of Bachelor of Elementary Education can make a significant impact on the lives of their students and contribute to the Philippine education system.




This comprehensive undergraduate program in the Philippines prepares graduates with the necessary skills and knowledge to pursue careers in police administration, jail management, and industrial security, with a curriculum covering criminal law, forensic science, and criminology theories, allow- ing them to pursue professions such as police offi- cers, detectives, and security officers, and to take the Criminologist Licensure Examination to become licensed criminologists, ultimately help- ing to promote public safety, reduce crime rates, and ensure justice and fairness for all.






This undergraduate program focuses on develop- ing students' skills and knowledge in accounting and financial management. It covers topics such as financial accounting, cost accounting, taxation, auditing, and financial reporting. Graduates are equipped to handle financial records, analyze and interpret financial statements, and develop accounting systems for businesses and organiza- tions. They are also prepared for professional cer- tification exams such as Certified Public Accoun- tant (CPA) or Chartered Accountant (CA), which lead to job opportunities in public and corporate accounting firms, as well as government agencies.





This undergraduate program is tailored to prepare students for a diverse range of careers in the busi- ness world. It provides a strong foundation in busi- ness and management principles, including finan- cial and human resource management. Graduates are equipped with analytical skills to manage bud- gets, make sound financial decisions, and develop strategies for managing human resources. They can pursue careers in various fields, such as bank- ing, finance, accounting, and human resources.
